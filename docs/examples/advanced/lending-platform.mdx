This example shows how to model a lending platform's chart of accounts in Formance — connecting the lenders who fund loans, the borrowers who draw and repay them, and the per-loan books that track principal and interest separately. The schema focuses on the **account structure**: where capital sits as it moves from a lender's available pool into a deployed loan and back through repayments.

**Common use cases:**
- **Peer-to-peer lending** matching lender funding pools to borrower loans
- **Marketplace lending** tracking outstanding principal and accrued interest per loan
- **Revenue-based financing** separating principal recovery from interest income

<DocCallout type="info">
This is an illustrative example focused on the chart of accounts. Adapt the schema to your specific business requirements, regulatory obligations, and financial practices.
</DocCallout>

## Key Concepts

- **Principal vs Interest** — Each borrower's debt is split into a `principal` account and an `interest` account, keyed by `$borrower_id`. Keeping them apart lets you recognize interest income on its own schedule and report outstanding principal independently of accrued interest. The `borrower_principal` metadata type tags the principal accounts as a class.
- **Lender Capital Lifecycle** — A lender's capital lives in two accounts keyed by `$lender_id`: `available` is uncommitted cash ready to deploy, `deployed` is capital currently funding loans. Money moves from `available` to `deployed` at origination and back as borrowers repay.
- **Per-Loan Books** — Each loan, keyed by `$loan_id`, carries an `outstanding` balance (what is still owed) and a `payments` account (what has been received). This makes the live state of any single loan explicit and auditable.

## Chart of Accounts

The `chart` section defines three account groups: borrowers, lenders, and loans.

### Borrowers

<LedgerSchema schema="lending-platform" chart="borrowers" />

Keyed by `$borrower_id`. `principal` holds the outstanding loan principal the borrower owes; `interest` accrues interest charged on it. Splitting the two keeps principal recovery and interest income separately reportable. The `borrower_principal` metadata type marks the account class.

### Lenders

<LedgerSchema schema="lending-platform" chart="lenders" />

Keyed by `$lender_id`. `available` is the lender's uncommitted capital; `deployed` is capital currently out in loans. Origination shifts funds from `available` to `deployed`; repayment returns them.

### Loans

<LedgerSchema schema="lending-platform" chart="loans" />

Per-loan books keyed by `$loan_id`. `outstanding` tracks the balance still owed on the loan; `payments` records amounts received against it. A zero `outstanding` means the loan is fully repaid.
